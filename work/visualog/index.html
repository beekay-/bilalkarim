Process

I designed and developed Visualog as a personal project. Because location plays an important role in determining my curiosity, the application had to be very map-centric. Moves played an important role in gathering the data through iPhone 7. At the end of the year, I exported all the data as GeoJSON and compiled it into a database that I could use for this project. I wanted to experiment with two contrasting styles of maps (dark versus light) while keeping the colours of movement and places the same (i.e., the data layer). I was able to build most of the functionality using Flask, MongoDB, Apache, Google Maps API, and Google Cloud Platform.

Challenges

There were several challenges experienced during the development of this project.

Continous tracking. Using Moves to continously track myself was a challenge, primarily due to battery issues. Although Moves had the ability to turn tracking on and off, I had to remember or build a habit to keep tracking on. There were times when I did not want tracking on.

Data performance. 341 days of data is a lot. I struggled with loading all of the data on the map and then styling it. One way I solved it was by sending one request for data instead of two, and by using GZIP compression for the first time. I had to look into it.

Additionally, good contrast is needed to highlight the data. Initially I wanted the map style to reflect time of the day, but struggled with technical development.

Future ideas

Lots of ideas did not make it to the first version of this project. For example, I'd love to be able to browse multi-year and look at my travels and places visited. Although photos exist on a separate page on Visualog, it would be better to incorporate them in the map as well with deeper photos integration to tell the complete story of the journey. I also initally wanted to play my routes on Google Street View, similar to Teehan project but couldn't due to Google Maps API limitations. It's something I would like to experiment given the recent changes in pricing of Maps API.
