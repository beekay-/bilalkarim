Problem

Existing energy efficiency solutions don’t motivate homeowners to make improvements. As a homeowner, it’s difficult to fix a problem you can’t see.

Solution

Create a responsive web application with Heat Loss Maps and Ratings at its core to visually reveal inefficiencies inside and around homes, communities, and cities.

Objectives

Engage and educate homeowners with their home’s heat loss, and connect them with local service providers and incentives to help improve their home’s comfort, savings, and energy efficiency.

Involvement

As a Product Design Lead, I oversee the entire product lifecycle of MyHEAT’s responsive web application—everything from design to full-stack development—using an agile process. This includes creation of wireframes, pixel-perfect mockups, rapid prototypes, and code optimization to deliver a unified user experience across all platforms.

Results

MyHEAT’s responsive web application is currently available for over 1.2 million homes across 15 Canadian cities (with growing interest from US).

Actively engaged audience

Increased social media sharing

High conversion ratio for industry

The objectives to engage, educate, and connect are supported by an actively engaged audience with session duration of over 4 minutes, increased social media sharing and viral activity, and a high conversion ratio when compared to the industry average.

MyHEAT also received extensive media coverage from Google, Global News, and was recently nominated for Canadian Innovation Awards by Techvibes.

Challenges

Several challenges were experienced during each phase of the product lifecycle. Although significant improvements were made over time, a digital product is never finished. The solutions to these challenges will always need some refinement.

Demographics. Homeowners vary across multiple age groups—from 25-34 to 65+. How does one create a responsive web application that is universally intuitive and easy-to-understand regardless of the age group? Furthermore, the perception of energy efficiency may also vary from city to city. How does one accomodate for those changes in perception throughout the tone and language of MyHEAT?

Data complexity. Data themselves, namely the Heat Loss Maps, are complex to begin with. How does one guide the user as what they’re seeing in their home’s Heat Loss Map? Through the use of summary cards or FAQ-style articles? Additionally, due to the nature of these data, size can also be issue which can affect development time in terms of loading performance, and impact the user experience of the application.

Sponsorship. As more sponsorships and partnerships are formed, how does one support the placement of these without compromising the user experience and not making it seem like an advertisement?
