MyHEAT

Helping millions visualize home heat loss for improved comfort, savings, and energy efficiency.

Visit Site

Problem

Existing energy efficiency solutions do not motivate homeowners to make improvements. As a homeowner, it is difficult to fix a problem you cannot see.

Solution

Build a responsive web application that visually showcases homeowners where energy is escaping from their homes, communities, and cities, and what to do about it.

Objectives

Engage and educate homeowners about their home’s heat loss. Then, connect homeowners with local service providers and energy efficiency programs to help improve their home’s comfort and increase energy savings.

Involvement

As a Product Design Lead, I oversaw the entire product lifecycle of MyHEAT’s responsive web application—from design to full stack development—using an agile process. This included continuous refinement through creation of wireframes, high fidelity mockups, and rapid prototypes. I also worked on code and asset optimization for delivering a unified user experience across all platforms.

Results

During my tenure, MyHEAT’s responsive web application grew to profiling of over 1.2 million homes across 18 Canadian cities (with growing interest from US). The objectives to engage, educate, and connect homeowners were supported by an actively engaged audience with session duration of over 4 minutes, increased social media sharing and viral activity, and a high conversion ratio compared to the industry average.

Additional products

I led the design and development on two additional products besides the responsive web application. Firstly, I developed an API for utilities to integrate MyHEAT data within their customer portals. Secondly, I created a web-based internal QA tool to help improve MyHEAT’s data quality.

Challenges

There were several challenges during each phase of the product lifecycle. Although significant improvements were made over time, a digital product is never finished. The solutions to these challenges will always need some refinement.

Privacy. The idea of revealing heat loss information of all homes in a city can cause privacy concerns. What design strategies and policies does one implement to ensure privacy of individual homes?

Sponsorship. As more partnerships are formed, how does one support the placement of their messaging without compromising the user experience and not make it seem like plain advertisement?

Demographics. Homeowners vary across multiple age groups—from 25-34 to 65+. How does one create a responsive web application that is universally intuitive and easy-to-understand? Furthermore, the perception of energy efficiency may also vary from city to city. How does one accomodate for those changes in tone and language of the product?

Data complexity. Data themselves, namely the Heat Loss Maps, are complex to interpret. How does one guide the user as what they are seeing in their home’s Heat Loss Map? Through the use of summary cards or FAQ-style articles? Additionally, due to complexity these data, size can impact performance and the user experience of the application.

Ultimately, all of these affect the usability and accessibility of MyHEAT’s responsive web application, which can impact its success metrics and objectives.
